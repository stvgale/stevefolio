Discovery

We met with Producto owners to gather information they had about the product, and its multiple modules. We then dove into sitemaps and existing user flows to discover 8 different modules with various user types. Each layout was broken down to its features and documented. We were then provided with hours of recorded user interviews and worked with research to understand their work processes, pain points and how much time they actually spend in the system. Main takeaways

  • Too many unesessary clicks to complete a task
  • Hidden data creates a problem
  • users opening and closing multiple tabs to keep different sections in view

UX Design

During this stage, I sketched out many various versions of the prototype I wanted to build. As I started the sketches, I kept learning more myself about the functionality of the app, and iterated on the spot. The user flow in this app is extremely important and needs to be absolutely clear to the end user because it can be such an immersive function of their daily lives. While prototyping this app, it gave me a great sense of finding the value to a user and I redid many steps along the way to make it more streamlined and less cluttered.

Test

We were given a great opportunity to test our prototypes and get feedback from real users over the course of the project.

Key takeaways

  • - Lots of enthusiasm for new design
  • - The new layout is intuitive for people used to the system, and for new users who are familiar with Microsoft (almost all)
  • - Want personalization and interactivity for dashboards
  • -Actionable feedback and direction on Mobile Use Cases
  • - Very useful feedback on granular UI details
  • - Good contacts for additional testing and research - customers are excited about being part of the process, once they understand the value of UX
  • Top of Mind for Attendees
  • - Reports - make them better
  • - Integrations - highly desired
  • - BI - how much will be available "Out of the Box"
  • - Document features
  • Collaborating
  • Seeing "Change Only" view
